How We Do Tradeshows

       

The big picture — strategy, philosophy, and how an event gets approved.

     
   
   
      ❌ The Wrong Strategy: Show up, walk the floor, collect badges, hope for the best. You're competing with 500+ vendors for the same attention. This doesn't work.    
   
      ✅ Our Strategy: Treat the conference as the in-person component of a 4-week outreach campaign. By the time you land, your meetings are already booked. The event is where you deepen relationships and move conversations forward — not where deals close, but where the groundwork for closing gets laid.

Pre-Event

       

The 4-week outreach campaign that fills your calendar before you arrive.

     
   
   
      🎯 Your pre-event goal: Book a minimum of 3 onsite meetings before you travel. The conference floor is where you meet — not where you prospect.    
   
     

📅 The 4-Week Pre-Event Timeline

     
       
         
T – 4 Weeks
         

Set ROI Goals & Divide the Work

         

ROI Planning — SueJean and the segment lead align on the event's ROI target, working backwards from the cost of attendance to determine how many deals need to close to justify the investment.

         

Attendee List — SueJean provides the attendee list. The segment lead and sales team sift through it using their ICP filter, then divide and conquer — each rep owns their slice with no overlap and no gaps.

         

Prioritization — Filter by role, company size, industry, and buying signals. Lead with warm accounts, existing pipeline, and known contacts first.

       
       
         
T – 3 Weeks
         

Launch Outreach

         

Short, direct, human. Use the template below as a starting point.

       
       
         
T – 2 Weeks
         

Post on Socials

         

Let your network know you'll be at the show. Post on LinkedIn and other relevant channels, and include a link to your calendar so people can book time to meet with you at the event.

       
       
         
T – 1 Week
         

Follow Up + Lock In Calendar Slots

         
               
  • Send a follow-up to non-responders
    •            
  • Book confirmed meetings in your calendar with location details
    •            
  • Confirm times with anyone who replied but didn't commit
    •            
  • Review SueJean's event guide for logistics, booth details, and schedule
    •          
       
       
         
Event Week
         

Execute

         

You're here to run meetings, not find them. See the At Event section.

At the Event

       

Execute your pre-booked meetings and capture every lead properly.

     
   
   
      Mindset check: You're not here to close deals or wander the floor collecting badges. You're here to run the meetings you already set up, deepen relationships, and move each conversation one step closer to a close.    
   
     

📱 Lead Capture — The Most Important Process

     

Every person you talk to must be captured in the lead capture app and tagged correctly. This is non-negotiable — untagged leads can't be followed up properly.

     
       
         
         

🔴 Red

         

Meeting booked or requested. Sales owns this lead. No automation — red leads require personal, handheld follow-up directly from the rep.

       
       
         
         

🟡 Yellow

         

Interested, not ready. Added to an automated sequence prompting them to book a meeting.

       
       
         
         

🔵 Blue

         

Cold lead. Added to our newsletter to stay on the radar long-term.

       
     
     
     

Lead Capture Flow

     
       
         
1

Use the lead capture app during or immediately after the conversation

Post-Event

       

Where the ROI is made. Speed and consistency in follow-up wins deals.

     
   
   
      Speed matters: Follow up within 24–48 hours while you're still top of mind. Every day you wait, the signal decays.    
   
     

🔄 Post-Event Data Flow SueJean David

     
       
         
Step 1
         

SueJean Transfers Lead Data

         

Pulls all captured leads from the lead capture app, organizes by Red/Yellow/Blue, and hands off to David Starr.

       
       
         
Step 2
         

David Starr Uploads to HubSpot

         

All leads uploaded with correct tags, notes, and source attribution. Automated sequences triggered for Yellow leads. Blue leads added to newsletter.

       
       
         
Step 3 ⚠️ Important
         

Sales Follows Up on Red Leads

         

Red leads are handheld — they are not added to any automation. Sales must personally send each red lead a follow-up email post-show within 48 hours. If a meeting was requested, it should already be on the calendar — confirm and prep.

       
       
         
Ongoing
         

SueJean Monitors Pipeline

         

SueJean pulls a report on leads and meetings and tracks whether pipeline grows in the weeks and months following the event.
